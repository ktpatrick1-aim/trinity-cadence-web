The Trinity Cadence Partner Promise

Our Commitment to Practitioners
I built Trinity Cadence because I needed it — as a Fractional COO and Integrator running cadence with my own clients. When I decided to make it available to other practitioners, I made myself a promise: the tool would support your work, never compete with it. This document puts that promise in writing.
Six Commitments
1

No Direct Consulting to Partner Clients

Trinity One Consulting will not offer Fractional COO, Integrator, or consulting services to any client organization brought onto Trinity Cadence by a partner — for the duration of the partner relationship and 12 months after.

2

No Poaching

Trinity Cadence will not market consulting, facilitation, or implementation services directly to organizations with an active partner relationship. Product communications (feature updates, billing) are the only outbound contact.

3

Transparent Attribution

Partner-referred organizations are permanently tagged in the system. Attribution is visible to both the partner and Trinity Cadence. There is no mechanism to quietly reassign a client.

4

Client Continuity

If a partner disengages from a client, the client retains full access to Trinity Cadence at standard per-seat rates. No lock-in through the partner. No data loss. The client's history, Blueprint, Pulse data, and Playbooks remain intact.

5

Partner-First Communication

Product changes, pricing updates, and roadmap shifts are communicated to partners before they reach partner clients. Partners are never surprised by a change their client sees first.

6

Your Methodology, Your Relationship

Trinity Cadence is a platform, not a methodology. Partners bring their own facilitation style, coaching approach, and client relationship. Trinity Cadence supports the work — it does not replace the practitioner.

This isn't a legal contract. It's a professional commitment from one practitioner to another. If you have questions about how this applies to a specific situation, I'm a phone call away.
