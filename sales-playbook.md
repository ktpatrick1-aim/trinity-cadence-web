# Trinity Cadence — Sales Playbook

## Overview

This playbook covers two sales channels:
- **Channel 1:** Integrators/Implementers (B2B2C partner channel)
- **Channel 2:** Direct to companies (B2C)

---

## Channel 1: Integrator Discovery Questions

Use these in a peer-to-peer demo conversation — not a sales pitch.

1. **How many client organizations are you managing right now?**
   - Sizing for fractional tier recommendation (Starter ≤3, Professional ≤8, Practice 9+)

2. **What tool are you using today for weekly meetings and scorecards?**
   - Identifies current platform and switching cost

3. **What are you paying per seat across all your client orgs?**
   - Opens the flat-rate pricing conversation — this is the hook

4. **How do you currently onboard a new client onto your platform?**
   - Reveals friction points Trinity Cadence can solve

5. **What's your biggest frustration with your current tool?**
   - Surfaces unmet needs — typically: cost, dated UI, no AI, trademark constraints

6. **Do you document processes with your clients? How?**
   - Opens the Playbook templates conversation

7. **How do you track what happened in a client org between your visits?**
   - Introduces the audit log and AI Monday Briefing

---

## Channel 2: Direct Company Discovery Questions

1. **Are you running a business operating system or framework today?**
   - Qualifies whether they're EOS-aware or net-new

2. **Who facilitates your weekly leadership meetings?**
   - Identifies whether they have an Integrator (potential Channel 1 lead)

3. **What tools are you using for scorecards, priorities, and issues?**
   - Common answers: spreadsheets, Ninety.io, nothing formal

4. **What's broken about your current cadence?**
   - Surfaces pain: meetings run long, priorities slip, same issues recur

5. **How does your team track 90-day priorities today?**
   - Anchors are often the biggest "aha" feature

6. **Do you have documented core processes? How current are they?**
   - Opens the Playbook value conversation

## Top 5 Objection Responses

### 1. "We already use Ninety.io"

**Response framework:**
> "That's great — you already have the discipline of a weekly cadence. Two questions: How much are you paying per seat, and what does the AI do for you during your meetings?"

**Key points:**
- Ninety.io charges per-seat. For a 10-person team: ~$200/month on Ninety.io vs. $160-$240/month on Trinity Cadence (comparable). The difference is what you GET.
- Trinity Cadence has 6 native AI features. Ninety.io's AI is limited.
- For Integrators: Ninety.io charges per-seat across every client org. Trinity Cadence is flat-rate.
- Trinity Cadence uses proprietary vocabulary — no trademark dependency.

### 2. "The terminology is different from what we know"

**Response framework:**
> "Every concept maps 1:1 to what you already know. We built a Translation Guide right into the product — click one button and you see the familiar terms alongside ours. Your team learns the new names in their first Huddle. The reason we use our own vocabulary is that it gives us freedom to evolve the product without trademark constraints."

**Key points:**
- Show the Translation Guide in the demo
- Emphasize: same concepts, same workflow, different (and often clearer) names
- Trademark freedom means we can innovate without licensing restrictions

### 3. "I've never heard of this product"

**Response framework:**
> "You're right — we're new to market. What we're not new to is running businesses. I built Trinity Cadence because I'm a Fractional COO who was frustrated with the tools I was asking my clients to use. I use this with my own clients every week. The product exists because I needed it."

**Key points:**
- Lead with KP's 30+ years of operational experience
- "Built by an Integrator who uses it every Monday morning"
- Offer to show a real (anonymized) client instance
- Being new means they get direct access to the founder for feedback and feature requests

### 4. "Can I see it in action?"

**Response framework:**
> "Absolutely. I can do one of two things: walk you through the product in a 30-minute demo using a real instance I use with my own clients, or you can start a 14-day free trial right now and I'll check in with you in a week."

**Key points:**
- Always offer both: live demo AND self-serve trial
- Live demo is preferred for Integrators (higher-touch, peer conversation)
- Self-serve trial is fine for companies (let the product sell itself)
- Calendly link: calendly.com/kevin-trinityoneconsulting

### 5. "What if you go away? You're a solo founder"

**Response framework:**
> "Fair question. Three things: First, the product runs on standard infrastructure — Supabase (Postgres), React, Netlify. There's no proprietary lock-in. Your data is always exportable. Second, my consulting business generates revenue independently of the software — I'm not burning through VC funding hoping to find product-market fit. Third, you're getting direct access to the founder. I'm not going anywhere, and neither is the consulting practice that funds this."

**Key points:**
- Standard tech stack = no proprietary risk
- Consulting revenue sustains the business independently
- No VC pressure or runway concerns
- Direct founder access is a feature, not a risk

## Closing Process

### For Companies

| Day | Action |
|-----|--------|
| 0 | Trial activated (14 days) |
| 1 | Welcome email (automated via MailerLite) |
| 3 | Check: Did they create a Blueprint and invite team? |
| 7 | **Check-in call** (15 min) — "How's the setup going? What questions do you have?" |
| 10 | AI feature preview email (upsell to Pro if on Cadence trial) |
| 12 | **Conversion call** (15 min) — "Your trial ends in 2 days. Ready to pick a plan?" |
| 14 | Trial expires. Follow-up email if not converted. |
| 21 | Final follow-up. If no response, archive and revisit in 90 days. |

### For Integrators

| Day | Action |
|-----|--------|
| 0 | Demo completed. Trial activated. |
| 1 | Send competitive one-pager + pricing math for their specific client count |
| 3 | **Onboarding call** (60 min) — Platform walkthrough |
| 7 | Help set up first client org (30 min) |
| 10 | Translation Guide review (15 min) |
| 14 | **First client Huddle** — offer to ride-along as silent observer |
| 30 | **30-day check-in** — feedback, testimonial ask, partner agreement if converting |

## Pricing Negotiation Guardrails

- **Annual billing discount (20% off) is the only pricing lever.** No custom pricing.
- **No free trial extensions beyond 14 days.** If they need more time, offer a demo call instead.
- **Fractional tier pricing is non-negotiable.** The math speaks for itself.
- **Volume discounts:** Not available in Phase 1. Revisit at 50+ subscribers.
- **Non-profit/education discount:** Not established yet. Handle case-by-case. Max 20% off.

7. **Partner Active** — Paying fractional tier, actively using with clients
8. **Founding Partner** — Formal agreement signed, roadmap input

## Trademark Reminders

**NEVER use these terms in external-facing sales materials:**
- L10, Level 10 Meeting
- Rocks
- Scorecard
- IDS
- V/TO, Vision/Traction Organizer
- People Analyzer
- GWC
- Accountability Chart
- Core Focus
- 10-Year Target
- 3-Year Picture
- Quarterly Conversations

**ALWAYS use Trinity Cadence vocabulary instead.** The Translation Guide exists inside the product for user reference, and that is the ONLY appropriate place for EOS-specific terms (clearly attributed to EOS Worldwide).
